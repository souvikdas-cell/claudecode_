## 10. Communication Learnings

### 10.1 Awareness and Discovery
- Generic awareness can increase visits.
- Awareness alone does not guarantee execution.
- What’s New and walkthrough campaigns can create first-time dashboard visits at scale.

### 10.2 Content Performance
- English users responded better than non-English users in awareness campaigns.
- Expiry-led messaging underperformed.
- Loss-framed messaging performed best.
- Strategy value proposition works better as **loss reduction** than as abstract feature promotion.

### 10.3 Channel Learnings
- WhatsApp can drive high CTR.
- But redirect leakage was significant.
- Plotline in-app bottom sheets improved operational reliability and page visits.
- Personalized P&L communication worked better for conversion than for broad discovery.

### 10.4 Durable Read
- Broad discovery and high-intent conversion should be treated separately.
- Best communication use cases:
  1. Discovery for F&O active non-users
  2. Personalized reactivation for loss-making traders
  3. In-app nudges at moments of trading intent

## 11. UX and Clarity Gaps

### 11.1 Durable UX Truth
The core problem is not no intent.
The core problem is **confusion before final action**.

### 11.2 Repeated Patterns Seen
- Users explore Strategy Builder, Basket, charts, payoff, Greeks.
- Users switch strikes and widgets.
- Users often do not know the final step to place order.
- Users confuse Strategy Builder with recommendations or tips.
- Users need explanation of what the tool does and what to do next.

### 11.3 Durable Product Requirements
- First-time walkthroughs are mandatory.
- Better CTA naming is mandatory.
- Simplify “next step” visibility on Basket and Strategy flows.
- Reduce to-and-fro between Option Chain and Editor.
- Improve mobile legibility and clickable area size.
- Education is a product feature here, not only a marketing support layer.

## 14. Durable Product Conclusions

### 14.1 What is true right now
- StrikeX is an **app-led** product.
- **Web is materially behind** in both adoption and conversion.
- **Strategy Builder** is the strongest current module by historical pull.
- **Strategy Finder** may reduce confusion for first-time users.
- **Basket** shows demand but not enough downstream proof yet.
- **Retention is weak**.
- Most users try StrikeX once and do not build a habit.
- Order volume likely depends on a small heavy-trader cohort.
- Measurement quality is uneven, especially on web.
- Product problem is not just access.
- Product problem is **confidence, clarity, and repeat value**.
- Communication can accelerate usage, but onboarding clarity is a deeper lever than campaign scale.

## 15. Strategic Implication for FinX / StrikeX

### 15.1 Priority order
1. **Web adoption first for AMJ**
2. **Web conversion second for AMJ**
3. **Retention before breadth**
4. **First-time clarity before feature expansion**
5. **Strategy Builder, Price Ladder, and Option Chain as primary web entry levers**
6. **Instrumentation cleanup before over-reading minor KPI swings**

### 15.2 Practical Build Order
1. Make web entry points visible
2. Make first-time use understandable
3. Make best modules easier to reach
4. Improve repeat-use loops
5. Then expand breadth
